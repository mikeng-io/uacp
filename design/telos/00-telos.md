## Purpose (the end)
UACP exists to **reduce the long-run friction of cooperation** on work done by *semantic*
(non-deterministic) actors — agents, and the humans who direct them.

Friction here is **time-asymmetric**, and the telos only makes sense stated that way:

- **At the point of interaction, governance ADDS friction.** Declaring intent, passing gates,
  producing evidence, thinking about process — the human and the agent both feel that cost *now*.
- **Over the pipeline's lifetime, governance REMOVES friction.** The loop leaves guardrails,
  SOPs, and a serialized record behind it: later work runs on rails, is not re-derived, is not
  re-litigated, and does not silently drift.

So the telos is not "less friction" — the cost-ward reading that would rationalize stripping
governance. It is: **invest bounded up-front friction where it is repaid with interest over the
horizon of cooperation, and keep the trade net-positive.** The friction budget (40) is the
amortization test for that trade — and it is judged on the witness side, never self-graded.

## Coherence is the product; the telos is why it is worth its price
The CMS bundle says *"coherence is the product"* — and that stands. The machinery manufactures
**coherence**: a system consistent with itself, whose claims are bound to evidence. What the CMS
bundle left unstated is *why that product is worth manufacturing* — and that is the telos:
coherent state is what makes the long-run side of the friction trade pay out. Product and
purpose, not rivals.

## The mechanism, in one line
UACP is a **conformance loop**: it makes *"does the realized reality match the declared
intent?"* the governed atom, closed across the lifecycle and across runs — forced into loop
form by the **semantic differentia** (the executor can neither infer the spec nor certify its
own pass; see 10). It is neither a spec framework nor a test framework: those are the frozen
write-side and check-side snapshots of the one relation it governs.

## The memory substrate (not "memory")
UACP does not provide memory, and `12-serialize.md` is right that it is *not a Memory
framework*. What the loop leaves behind — typed, provenanced, serialized residue, run after
run — is the **memory substrate**: the foundation on which memory (lessons, generalization,
recall) is *built*. Substrate is the loop's product across runs (30); what grows on it is
outside the atom and honestly declared so (10, honest limits).

## Relationship to the CMS bundle
CMS (comprehend → measure → serialize) is the **coherence discipline chosen to serve this
purpose** — the loop instantiated at a single grain. **CMS derives from the telos, not the
reverse.** The CMS bundle already reclassified itself from "axiom" to *chosen discipline*; this
bundle supplies what a chosen discipline logically requires — the purpose it was chosen *for* —
and the name `measure` is deliberately kept (30; ADR-0018: the word is the intervention).
